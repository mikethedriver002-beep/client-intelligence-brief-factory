# Agency Pitch One-Pager

## Brief Factory

White-label weekly client intelligence briefs for agencies.

## The problem

Agencies are expected to keep clients informed, proactive, and confident. But weekly research, competitor monitoring, reputation checks, local opportunity tracking, and source-backed recommendations take time.

Most agencies either:

- send generic reporting dashboards
- rely on scattered notes from account managers
- miss useful competitor or market signals
- spend too much senior time turning research into client-ready language

## The offer

Brief Factory gives agencies a reviewed weekly intelligence packet for one client niche.

Each packet includes:

- executive summary
- top developments
- competitor, reputation, market, or opportunity signals
- why-it-matters notes
- recommended client actions
- source links and timestamps
- evidence notes
- client-safe caveats
- QA and client-readiness status
- delivery ZIP with editable markdown and HTML files

## What makes it different

Brief Factory is not a generic AI summary tool. It is a source-backed, human-review-first brief system designed for agencies that need usable client actions.

The output is built around this question:

```text
What should the agency tell the client this week, and what should the client do next?
```

## Best-fit agency types

- local SEO agencies
- reputation management agencies
- PR firms
- content marketing agencies
- fractional CMO teams
- small B2B marketing agencies
- niche newsletter or publisher teams that serve sponsors or clients

## First pilot

Start with one client niche for one month.

Pilot deliverables:

- weekly client intelligence brief
- source appendix
- QA report
- handoff email
- optional outreach-ready sample version
- optional agency sales sample version

## Simple sample offer

Send one client niche, 2 to 4 competitors, and the geography or market. I will build a sample brief so your team can judge the format before committing to a monthly pilot.

## Positioning line

Brief Factory helps agencies turn monitoring into client-ready actions without hiring another researcher.
