# Objection Handling

## "Is this just AI summaries?"

No. The value is not generic summarization. The value is source selection, evidence notes, client-safe caveats, QA status, and recommended actions written for a specific client niche.

Use this reply:

```text
Totally fair question. The product is not an AI news summary. It is a reviewed brief packet built around sources, evidence notes, caveats, and specific client actions. The goal is to help your agency decide what to tell the client and what to do next.
```

## "We already have dashboards."

Dashboards show metrics. Brief Factory explains what changed and what the client should do next.

Use this reply:

```text
That makes sense. I would not position this as a replacement for dashboards. It sits above them as a short action memo for client conversations. The dashboard says what happened in the account. The brief says what changed around the client and what the agency should recommend.
```

## "We can do this ourselves."

They can. The question is whether it is worth the time every week.

Use this reply:

```text
You absolutely can. The question is whether your team wants to spend recurring time finding sources, cleaning notes, writing the action memo, and packaging the evidence. Brief Factory is designed to take that recurring research and formatting work off the team while keeping your review layer in place.
```

## "How accurate is it?"

Accuracy comes from source links, evidence notes, timestamps, caveats, and human review.

Use this reply:

```text
Every packet includes source links, evidence notes, timestamps, caveats, a source appendix, and QA status. It is not designed for blind forwarding. It is designed so your team can review quickly and decide what is client-safe.
```

## "Do you scrape private or restricted sources?"

No.

Use this reply:

```text
No. The pilot is built around public sources, manual observations, and agency-approved inputs. We avoid private, login-gated, restricted, or paywalled sources unless the agency has a clear approved workflow for that source.
```

## "Our clients are too niche."

That can be a strength.

Use this reply:

```text
Niche clients are usually a better fit because the brief is not trying to monitor the whole internet. It works best when we define a specific client, market, competitor set, and watchlist.
```

## "Can this be white-labeled?"

Yes. That is the point.

Use this reply:

```text
Yes. The output is designed as a white-label agency packet. Your team can edit it, forward it, or use it internally for client strategy.
```

## "What do we need to start?"

Use this reply:

```text
For a sample, I only need one client niche, the market or geography, 2 to 4 competitors, and a few watch topics. From there, I can produce a sample packet so you can judge whether it is useful.
```

## "What happens after the sample?"

Use this reply:

```text
If the sample is useful, we turn it into a one-month paid pilot: one client niche, one weekly brief, source appendix, QA report, and handoff packet each week. After that, we decide whether to expand to more clients.
```
