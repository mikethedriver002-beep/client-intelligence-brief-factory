# Sales Process SOP

## Objective

Close the first paid Brief Factory pilot.

## Offer to sell first

```text
One-month paid pilot for one agency client niche.
```

Default pilot:

```text
$500 for one month
4 weekly briefs
1 client niche
source appendix and QA report included
agency reviews before client forwarding
```

## Daily sales rhythm

Monday:

- add 5 to 10 prospects to the tracker
- score each prospect
- personalize first lines

Tuesday:

- send 5 cold emails
- send 5 LinkedIn connection requests

Wednesday:

- send 5 more emails
- follow up with interested replies
- schedule sample calls if needed

Thursday:

- build one sample packet for the strongest interested prospect
- ask for feedback

Friday:

- follow up
- ask for a paid pilot
- update the prospect tracker

## First call agenda

1. Ask what clients they serve.
2. Ask what reporting or strategy check-ins look like now.
3. Ask what they wish they monitored more consistently.
4. Ask for one client niche to test.
5. Explain the sample brief format.
6. Set expectations: reviewed packet, source links, caveats, no blind forwarding.
7. Ask if a one-month pilot makes sense if the sample is useful.

## Qualification questions

Ask:

- Do you already sell monthly retainers?
- Which clients require the most reporting or strategy support?
- Do account managers spend time researching competitors or market updates?
- Would weekly client-ready talking points help?
- Who would review a sample brief internally?
- What would make this worth paying for monthly?

## Sample build workflow

1. Collect onboarding answers.
2. Create client config.
3. Create source registry.
4. Create manual intake file with 3 to 6 items.
5. Run Brief Factory.
6. Inspect QA, source appendix, weekly brief, outreach sample, and sales sample.
7. Send packet with a short note.
8. Ask for feedback and pilot decision.

## Close language

Use this after they review a sample:

```text
If this format feels useful, I suggest we keep the pilot very simple: one client niche, one weekly brief, one month. Your team reviews each packet before forwarding anything. At the end of the month, we decide whether it saved enough time and created enough client value to continue.
```

## Follow-up after sample

```text
What was useful, what felt unnecessary, and would you want this weekly for one real client?
```

## Pilot success criteria

A pilot is successful if the agency says at least two of these:

- this saved research time
- this gave us better client talking points
- this surfaced a useful competitor or market signal
- this helped us look more proactive
- this could be used in a client check-in
- this could become a retainer add-on

## Do not do this yet

- Do not pitch a full SaaS platform.
- Do not promise full automation.
- Do not promise complete coverage.
- Do not offer unlimited sources.
- Do not accept a vague broad niche.
- Do not build a free custom strategy report with no pilot path.

## Win condition

The first win is:

```text
1 agency pays for 1 client niche for 1 month.
```
