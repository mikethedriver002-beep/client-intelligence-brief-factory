# Client Onboarding Questionnaire

Use this after an agency says yes to a sample or pilot.

## Agency basics

1. Agency name:
2. Main contact name:
3. Main contact email:
4. Agency website:
5. What services do you sell? SEO, PR, reputation, content, paid media, strategy, other?
6. Who will review the sample brief?

## Client basics

1. Client name:
2. Client website:
3. Client industry or niche:
4. Client geography or service area:
5. Is this a real client, sample client, or anonymized client?
6. What does the client sell?
7. What are the client's most important services or offers?

## Competitors and market

1. List 2 to 5 competitors:
2. Which competitor worries the client most?
3. Which market or city should we monitor first?
4. Which service areas matter most?
5. Are there seasonal events, deadlines, or demand cycles we should know about?

## Brief goals

What should this brief help the agency do?

- create better client check-ins
- find content angles
- monitor competitors
- spot reputation issues
- identify local opportunities
- support PR angles
- support sales or account strategy
- other

## Preferred signal types

Choose the most useful signals:

- competitor website changes
- Google Business Profile observations
- review/reputation movement
- local event or sponsorship opportunities
- industry news
- pricing or cost concerns
- hiring or expansion signals
- public notices or government updates
- client content gaps
- customer question trends

## Source preferences

1. Are there sources you trust most?
2. Are there sources you do not want used?
3. Should we include social posts as discovery-only leads?
4. Should we use manual observations from Google Business/Profile pages?
5. Should all source links be included in the final packet?

## Output preferences

1. Preferred cadence: weekly, biweekly, monthly?
2. Preferred delivery day:
3. Preferred brief length: short, standard, detailed?
4. Should the brief be written for internal agency use or client forwarding?
5. Should it include talking points for account managers?
6. Should it include client-safe caveats?
7. Should it include outreach/social/content suggestions?

## Approval rules

1. Who approves the brief before the client sees it?
2. Should all exact ratings, review counts, hours, prices, or deadlines be rechecked before client forwarding?
3. Are there any compliance or legal sensitivities?
4. Should client names be anonymized in sample materials?

## Success criteria

What would make this pilot worth paying for monthly?

- saves agency time
- creates better client conversations
- finds useful competitor signals
- helps with content strategy
- helps retain clients
- helps upsell clients
- improves reporting quality
- other

## Minimum info needed to start

At minimum, collect:

```text
agency name
client name or sample name
client website
client niche
client geography
2 to 4 competitors
3 to 5 watch topics
preferred delivery cadence
approval contact
```
